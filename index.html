James Newling
I'm a member of the software team at Graphcore, with a background in machine learning and observational cosmology. I obtained a PhD in computer science from EPFL in 2018. Here's my CV (pdf).

Education and experience

06/2020 - present. Software team lead. Graphcore. Bristol, UK. I work with a team developing PopART and Poprithms, components of the Poplar software stack.

03/2018 - 05/2020. Machine learning frameworks developer. Graphcore. Bristol, UK. I worked on the initial development of PopART and its integration with machine learning technologies such as ONNX and PyTorch.

01/2017 - 10/2017. Collaborator. Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) / HSA. Martigny, Switzerland. After my internship at AMD, I continued to collaborate on MIOpenGEMM . This was enabled through a grant from the HSA.

09/2016 - 12/2016. Co-op. Advanced Micro Devices (AMD). Austin, Texas. I was an intern in the team developing ROCm. I started the MIOpenGEMM project for optimized GEMM (matrix multiply) written in OpenCL and C++. More information can be found on the wiki.

09/2013 - 12/2017. Doctor of Philosophy (phD). École polytechnique fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL) Lausanne, Switzerland. I obtained a PhD in computer science from the EDIC doctoral school at the École polytechnique fédérale de Lausanne. I was advised by François Fleuret. My thesis was in unsupervised machine learning.

09/2013 - 12/2017. Research assistant. Idiap Research Institute. Martigny, Switzerland. I was a member of the Machine Learning Group at the Idiap Research Institute. Our group focused on statistical learning techniques for computer vision, with a special focus computational aspects. My main research was on partitional clustering, with code and papers available in the zentas and eakmeans repositories.

03/2013 - 08/2013. Research assistant. The Mukherjee Lab for Statistical Systems Biology. Amsterdam, The Netherlands. I was a visitor in the Mukherjee Lab for Statistical Systems Biology, at the Netherlands Cancer Institute, where I worked on non-parameteric statistical tests of drug efficacy. Here is my final report (pdf).

09/2011 - 08/2013. Masters in Complexity Science École polytechnique, University of Warwick Paris, France and Warwick, UK. A description of the program is here. I took courses in numerical ODEs and SDEs, random models in evolution, theoretical neuroscience, modern statistical inference, and others.

02/2006 - 10/2008, 02/2010 - 07/2011. Maths tutor and lecturer. University of Cape Town (UCT). Cape Town, South Africa. I tutored first year university mathematics at UCT, and later I was the course lecturer for a 4'th year non-linear optimization course.

11/2009 - 07/2011. MSc in Applied Mathematics University of Cape Town (UCT) Cape Town, South Africa A research masters degree in observational cosmology, funded by the SKA. My thesis was awarded a UCT Research Associateship.

04/2009 - 09/2009. School maths teacher. Kathmandu University High Scool (KUHS). Dhulikhel, Nepal. I taught grade 6 maths at the Kathmandu University High School.

02/2004 - 11/2008. Honours degree, Pure Mathematics and Statistics. University of Cape Town (UCT). Cape Town, South Africa.
